Here is the quantitative structural data extracted from the provided paper regarding the amylose tris(3,5-dimethylphenylcarbamate) (ADMPC) polymer.

### Helical Architecture and Unit Cell

* 
**Helical Type**: The polymer adopts a left-handed 4/3 helical structure.


* 
**Simulation Cell Dimensions**: For a 4-mer repeating unit under periodic boundary conditions, the simulation cell was defined with dimensions of x = 40 Å, y = 40 Å, and a helical pitch (z-axis) of z = 15.613 Å.



### Backbone Dihedral Angles

* 
**Definition**: The glycosidic bond linking the glucose monomers is defined by two specific dihedral angles: $\phi$ (H1-C1-O-C4') and $\psi$ (H4'-C4'-O-C1).


* 
**Optimal Conformation**: The lowest energy conformation that successfully builds the left-handed 4/3 helix occurs at the dihedral angles $\phi$ = -68.5° and $\psi$ = -42.0°.


* 
**Alternative Conformation**: A secondary energy minimum was observed near $\phi$ = +68° and $\psi$ = +42°, but this geometry was rejected because it produces a right-handed helix.



### Critical Interatomic Distances

* 
**Inter-residue Distance**: The distance between the H1 proton of one glucose unit and the H4' proton of the adjacent unit across the glycosidic bond was calculated to be between 2.83 Å and 2.96 Å.


* 
**Hydrogen Bonding (Complexation)**: When bound to the (S)-enantiomer of 1-(9-anthryl)-2,2,2-trifluoroethanol, the hydrogen bond distance between the analyte's hydroxyl proton and the C2 carbonyl oxygen of the ADMPC carbamate group is 1.968 Å.
